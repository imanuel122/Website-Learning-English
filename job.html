Job Interview: Tips, What to Dress, Common Formal Dress, Common Question & Technology in Interview

Understanding Job Interview

A job interview is a meeting between a company and an employee candidate. Companies will ask a number of things to find out whether the candidate is worthy of being their employee.
There are several steps in applying job. After sending the application letter, you will be called to attend job interview if you are chosen for next steps. Job interview is interview between applicant and employer to assess and decide whether he/she should be hired or not. It is very important step in your carrier so that you need to prepare it well.

Tips for Job Interview

• Do your research on the company and position you applied.
• Do rehearsal.
• Learn about body language.
• Dress properly from top-to-toe.
• Take meals before interview.
• Come on time.
• Smile and relax.

What to Dress for Job Interview

• Dressing appropriately for an interview is also very important.
• REMEMBER, you don't always have to wear a suit, you should always look your best. We recommend dressing one step above what you would typically wear every day in your job.
• So if you will be wearing business casual, we recommend you wear a suit to the interview. If you will be wearing jeans every day to your job, dressing in a button down shirt and khakis (for men) or dress pants and a nice blouse (for women)would be appropriate for the interview.
• FOR WOMEN: Avoid excessive make-up and jewelry, Do not use perfume or strong body washes/sprays, Do not wear clothing that is too tight or too short, Do not wear open toed shoes or stilettos, Carry either a professional, conservative purse or attaché case.
• FOR MEN: You should have clean, trimmed hair and facial hair; Do not use cologne or a strong smelling aftershave; If wearing a suit, choose gray, black, or navy with a coordinating shirt and professional tie; Empty your pockets; Wear a nice looking belt and matching shoes; Carry a leather portfolio or briefcase; Have clean, short nails; Wear dress socks and polished dress shoes.

Common Question

• Please tell me about yourself
• Tell me what you know about this company.
• Why do you want to work here?
• What are your strengths?
• What are your weaknesses?
• What are your achievements?
• What did you like or dislike about your last job?
• What did you quit from your previous company?
• Where do you see yourself in five years?
• How will you handle stress and pressure in the office?
• Why should we hire you?
• What can we expect from you in your first three months?

Technology in Interview

Recently, due to pandemic, interviews using technology becoming more common through a telephone interview and through videoconferencing than face-to-face. This kind of interview needs your ability in using technology like having videoconferencing using Google Meet, Zoom, Webex or so on. Although this is cheaper and more effective, many employer still prefer face-to-face interview.
